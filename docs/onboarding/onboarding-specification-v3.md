# Onboarding Flow Specification v3

## Overview

A strategic, engaging 4-page onboarding that gathers data to power personalized scoring while immediately showing creators **why SuperTopics is different** and **how it will help them succeed**.

**Core Principle**: Your Tool. Your Data. Your Success.

---

## The Philosophy

### We Don't Give Generic Advice

> "Most tools give everyone the same numbers. The same 'search volume.' The same recommendations. SuperTopics is different. Everything is personalized to YOU - your niche, your audience, your goals."

### This is a Learning System

> "The more you use SuperTopics, the smarter it gets. We learn from your uploads, what's working, and what's not. This isn't static data - it's a system that grows WITH your channel."

### Our Data is Different

> "Our metrics come directly from YouTube - not third-party estimates. We augment it with AI, score it based on YOUR context, and surface opportunities that generic tools miss entirely."

## Page 4: Your Audience

### Purpose
Create a SPECIFIC, DETAILED description of their target viewer that powers precise Audience Fit scoring.

### The Critical Importance of Specificity

> "The more general the audience description, the less powerful the scores will be."

**Vague (weak scoring):**
- "People interested in YouTube"
- "Tech enthusiasts"
- "Anyone who wants to learn"

**Specific (powerful scoring):**
- "Small YouTubers with under 1,000 subscribers who are struggling to get views and want to understand the algorithm"
- "iPhone users who want to get better photos without learning professional photography"
- "Busy professionals who want to learn Japanese cooking in under 30 minutes"

## The Creator Context Summary

### What It Is

After completing onboarding, we generate a 3-5 sentence summary that captures EVERYTHING the AI needs to score Audience Fit accurately.

### How It's Generated

```typescript
async function generateCreatorContext(onboardingData: OnboardingData): Promise<string> {
  const prompt = `
Based on this creator's onboarding data, write a 3-5 sentence summary that captures:
1. Their core niche and content pillars
2. Their primary goal (why they create content)
3. The types of videos they make and format preferences
4. Their target audience and expertise level

Data:
- Niche: ${onboardingData.niche}
- Pillars: ${onboardingData.content_pillars.join(', ')}
- Primary Goal: ${onboardingData.primary_goal}
- Video types: ${onboardingData.video_types.join(', ')}
- Show first impressions opportunities: ${onboardingData.show_first_impressions}
- Show quick tips opportunities: ${onboardingData.show_quick_tips}
- Target audience: ${onboardingData.target_audience}
- Expertise level: ${onboardingData.audience_expertise}

Write a natural, concise summary that could be used as context for AI scoring.
Include their business goal as it affects what topics will serve them best.
`;

  const response = await openai.chat.completions.create({
    model: "gpt-4o-mini",
    messages: [{ role: "user", content: prompt }],
    max_tokens: 300,
  });

  return response.choices[0].message.content;
}
```

### Example Output

**Input:**
- Niche: "YouTube Education"
- Pillars: ["Algorithm & Discovery", "Content Creation", "AI Tools"]
- Primary Goal: "sell_products"
- Video types: ["explainers", "tips", "reviews"]
- Show first impressions: true
- Show quick tips: true
- Target audience: "Small YouTubers with under 1,000 subscribers who are struggling to get views"
- Expertise: "beginner"

**Generated Summary:**
> "This creator runs a YouTube Education channel focused on helping small creators understand the algorithm, improve their content, and leverage AI tools. Their primary goal is to sell their own products or courses, so topics that build trust and demonstrate expertise are ideal. They make explainer videos, tips content, and reviews, and are open to first impressions and quick tips formats for capturing opportunities faster. Their target audience is beginner YouTubers with under 1,000 subscribers who are struggling to get views and want to grow their channel."

### How It's Used in Scoring

```typescript
// In Audience Fit scoring prompt
```

## How Motive Affects Scoring

The `primary_goal` field allows us to weight topics differently:

| Primary Goal | Topics to Prioritize | Topics to Deprioritize |
|--------------|---------------------|----------------------|
| **Grow Audience** | Trending, broad appeal, entertainment | Niche buyer-intent |
| **AdSense Revenue** | High CPM niches, long watch time | Quick viral one-offs |
| **Sell Products** | Problem-aware, trust-building, pain-point | Pure entertainment |
| **Affiliate** | Reviews, comparisons, buyer-intent | Non-commercial content |
| **Authority** | Evergreen, expertise-demo, thought leadership | Trend-chasing |

This data flows into the Creator Context Summary and affects Audience Fit scores.

## Summary: The Onboarding Promise

After completing onboarding, we can confidently score ANY phrase for Audience Fit because we know:

| We Know | How It Helps Scoring |
|---------|---------------------|
| Their niche + pillars | Is this topic relevant to their channel? |
| Their primary goal | Does this topic serve their business objective? |
| Video formats they make | Could they actually create this video? |
| Format flexibility | Would a first impressions work? Quick tips? |
| Specific audience description | Would their viewers search for this? |
| Audience expertise level | Is this too basic? Too advanced? |

**The Result**: Creators get scores that reflect whether a topic fits THEIR channel AND their goals - not just whether it's a good topic in general.
