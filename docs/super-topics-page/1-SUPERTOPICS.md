## What is a SuperTopic?

A **SuperTopic** is a video topic that represents an exceptional opportunity for a specific creator. It's not just a "good keyword" - it's a topic where multiple signals align to create outsized potential.

**The key insight:** A SuperTopic is personal. What's a SuperTopic for one creator may not be for another.

---

## The Philosophy

### SuperTopics Are Contextual

A topic becomes a SuperTopic based on:

1. **The Creator's Goals** - What are they trying to achieve?
2. **The Creator's Channel** - Does this fit their audience?
3. **The Market Reality** - Is there demand? Is there room to compete?
4. **The Timing** - Is this evergreen or trending?

### Not Just High Volume

Traditional SEO tools chase high-volume keywords. But high volume often means:
- High competition
- Established players
- Hard to break through

**SuperTopics finds the gaps** - topics with real demand but low competition.

### Not Just Low Competition

Low competition alone isn't enough. A phrase nobody searches for has "low competition" but zero opportunity.

**SuperTopics require demand** - real people searching, watching, subscribing.

## Ways a Topic Becomes a SuperTopic

### 1. The Sweet Spot Pattern üéØ

**Low exact match + High topic match + High suggestions**

This means:
- People ARE searching for this topic (demand exists)
- But NOT with these exact words (low competition)
- And the topic is unified (semantic coherence)

**Example:** "How to introduce yourself on YouTube"
- 14 suggestions (maximum demand)
- 7% exact match (almost no competition)
- 100% topic match (everyone wants the same thing)
- **Result:** #1 ranking, 30K views, major subscriber growth

This is a **SuperTopic** because the algorithm detected something rare: maximum demand with minimal competition.

### 2. The Buying Intent Pattern üí∞

**Low demand + High buying intent + Low competition**

For creators selling products/services, a phrase with:
- Lower search volume
- But clear purchase intent
- And few competitors

...is more valuable than a high-volume phrase with no intent.

**Example:** "best CRM for YouTube creators"
- Maybe only 50 searches/month
- But those 50 people are READY TO BUY
- Few videos target this specifically
- **SuperTopic for someone selling to creators**

### 3. The Long-Term Views Pattern üìà

**Evergreen topic + Room to rank + Compounds over time**

Some topics aren't trending - they're permanent. People will search for them forever:
- "How to..." tutorials
- Beginner guides
- Foundational concepts

A phrase that:
- Has consistent (not spiking) demand
- You can rank for
- Will bring views for years

...is a SuperTopic because it compounds.

**Example:** "How to introduce yourself on YouTube"
- Not a trend - people ALWAYS start YouTube channels
- Ranked #1 within days
- Still getting views 10 months later
- **SuperTopic because it compounds**

### 4. The Audience Builder Pattern üë•

**High audience fit + Subscriber conversion potential**

Some topics attract viewers. Some topics attract **subscribers**.

A phrase where:
- The searcher matches your target audience exactly
- Watching this video leads to "I need more from this channel"
- High subscribe-after-watch rate

...is a SuperTopic even if volume is moderate.

**Example:** A channel about "starting a YouTube channel" publishing "how to introduce yourself on YouTube"
- Perfect audience match (new creators)
- Viewer thinks "this channel has what I need"
- Subscribes for the journey
- **SuperTopic because it builds the audience**

### 5. The Niche Domination Pattern üèÜ

**Tight niche + 100% topic relevance + Ownable**

Sometimes a phrase is so specific that:
- ALL suggestions are about the same thing
- You can become THE authority
- Once you rank, you own it

**Example:** "AI Thumbnail Roblox"
- Only 5 suggestions (niche)
- But 100% topic match (all about Roblox thumbnails)
- Tight, ownable niche
- **SuperTopic for the right creator**

When our algorithm calculates a phrase's potential, it produces a raw score:

| Raw Score | Designation | Meaning |
|-----------|-------------|---------|
| **100+** | üåü **SuperTopic** | Exceptional - all signals align |
| 70-99 | üü¢ Go | Strong opportunity |

| 20-34 | üü° Caution | Limited signals |
| <20 | üî¥ Stop | Low potential |

### How the Score is Calculated

```
Demand Score (0-40)
  + Topic Relevance Bonus (0-30)
  + Competition Score (-10 to +30)
  + Sweet Spot Bonus (0-15)
  = Raw Score
```

When raw score exceeds 100, the phrase has **exceeded expectations** across multiple dimensions. This earns the **SuperTopic** designation.

## The Demand Score Algorithm (0-100)

The Demand Score measures **how much people want this topic**. It's calculated using a Parent + Child formula.

### The Formula

```
Demand Score = Parent Points + Child Points
```

- **Parent Points:** How many suggestions did the parent phrase have? (0-50)
- **Child Points:** How many suggestions does this phrase have? (0-50)

For Level 1 (seed phrases with no parent): `Demand = Child Points √ó 2`

### Suggestion Count ‚Üí Points Mapping

Each suggestion count maps to points on a curve:

| Suggestions | Points | Notes |
|-------------|--------|-------|
| 14 | 50 | Maximum (YouTube shows max 14) |
| 13 | 47 | Near maximum |
| 12 | 44 | Very high |
| 11 | 40 | High |
| 10 | 36 | Strong |
| 9 | 32 | Good |
| 8 | 28 | Above average |
| 7 | 24 | Average |
| 6 | 20 | Below average |
| 5 | 16 | Low |
| 4 | 12 | Very low |
| 3 | 8 | Minimal |
| 2 | 4 | Almost none |
| 1 | 2 | Barely any |
| 0 | 0 | No suggestions |

## The Opportunity Score Algorithm (0-100)

The Opportunity Score is a multi-signal algorithm that evaluates a phrase's **ranking potential** - how likely you are to get views from this phrase. It combines six key factors:

### 0. Demand Base (0-30 points)

**Even Level 1 phrases deserve credit for high demand**

Every phrase starts with a baseline opportunity score based on its suggestion count. This ensures that even seed phrases (Level 1) show reasonable opportunity when demand is high.

| Suggestions | Points | Meaning |
|-------------|--------|---------|
| 12+ | 30 | Maximum demand |
| 10-11 | 25 | Very high demand |
| 7-9 | 18 | Good demand |
| 5-6 | 12 | Moderate demand |
| 3-4 | 5 | Low demand |
| 0-2 | 0 | Minimal demand |

**Why this matters:** A phrase like "YouTube video editing" with 14 suggestions should show opportunity ~30-40 even at Level 1, not 0.

### 1. Depth Boost (0-40 points)

**DEEPER = EASIER TO RANK = HIGHER OPPORTUNITY**

The deeper you can drill while maintaining suggestions, the easier the phrase is to rank for. Long-tail phrases are less competitive.

| Level | Points | Meaning |
|-------|--------|---------|
| Level 1 (first drill) | 8 | Starting to drill |
| Level 2 | 18 | Good depth |
| Level 3 | 28 | Great depth - easier to rank |
| Level 4+ | 35 | Maximum depth - very easy to rank |

**Bonus:** +5 if parent had 10+ suggestions (sustained demand)

**Example:** "YouTube video editing" ‚Üí "...tips" ‚Üí "...tips and tricks"
- Level 3 = +28 points + sustained demand bonus
- Easier to rank = higher opportunity

### 2. Sweet Spot Boost (0-25 points)

**Low exact match + High topic match = Hidden Opportunity**

This pattern means:
- People ARE searching for this topic (high topic match)
- But NOT using these exact words (low exact match)
- **Translation:** Demand exists, but few videos target this specific phrase

| Exact Match | Topic Match | Points | Meaning |
|-------------|-------------|--------|---------|
| ‚â§20% | ‚â•80% | 25 | Perfect Sweet Spot |
| ‚â§30% | ‚â•60% | 20 | Great Sweet Spot |
| ‚â§40% | ‚â•50% | 12 | Good pattern |
| ‚â§50% | ‚â•40% | 5 | Slight pattern |

**Example:** "How to introduce yourself on YouTube"
- 7% exact match = almost nobody using these words
- 100% topic match = everyone wants the same thing
- = 25 point Sweet Spot boost

### 3. Position Power (0-18 points)

**What is "Position"?**

When you drill down into a phrase, you click on one of the suggestions. That suggestion appeared at a specific position in the list (1st, 2nd, 3rd, etc.).

**Position 1 = YouTube's algorithm thinks this is THE most relevant expansion of that phrase.**

This is a strong signal because YouTube's autocomplete is powered by:
- What people actually search for
- What gets clicks
- What leads to satisfying results

**How Position Power Works:**

| Position in Parent's Suggestion List | Base Points | With 10+ Siblings |
|--------------------------------------|-------------|-------------------|
| #1 (top of list) | 15 | +3 bonus |
| #2 | 12 | +3 bonus |
| #3-4 | 8 | - |
| #5-7 | 4 | - |
| #8+ | 0 | - |

**The "10+ Siblings" Bonus:**
If the parent phrase had 10+ suggestions, being in the top 2 positions is extra meaningful - you beat out many competitors for that spot. +3 bonus.

**Example:**
You drilled into "YouTube video editing" and clicked "YouTube video editing course" which was listed **first** in the suggestions.
- Position = 1 ‚Üí 15 points
- Parent had 14 suggestions (10+) ‚Üí +3 bonus
- **Total Position Power = 18 points**

### 4. Relevancy Boost (0-20 points)

**The "Ranking Ladder" Concept**

When your child phrase **contains** the parent phrase, ranking for the specific child often helps you rank for the broader parent. This creates a ladder:

```
"YouTube video editing course free" (easiest to rank)
        ‚Üì helps you rank for
"YouTube video editing course" 
        ‚Üì helps you rank for
"YouTube video editing" (hardest to rank)
```

**How Relevancy Boost Works:**

| Relationship | Points |
|--------------|--------|
| Child starts with parent phrase exactly | 15 |
| Strong word overlap (but not exact start) | 10 |
| Also matches grandparent (full ladder) | +5 |

**Example:** 
- Grandparent: "YouTube video editing"
- Parent: "YouTube video editing course"
- Child: "YouTube video editing course free"

The child starts with the parent phrase exactly (+15) AND the full ladder goes back to grandparent (+5) = **20 points**

### 5. Long-Term Views Boost (0-26 points)

**Evergreen Topic Detection**

Some topics will drive views for years, not days. We detect these by looking for specific **evergreen keywords** that indicate educational, how-to, or foundational content.

**Evergreen Prefixes (start of phrase):**
```

what, what is, what are, what does, what do, what should
why, why is, why are, why does, why do, why should

**Evergreen Suffixes (anywhere in phrase):**
```

**How Long-Term Views Boost Works:**

| Pattern | Points |

| Has evergreen prefix OR suffix | 12 |
| Above + has 10+ suggestions | 18 |
| "how to" prefix or "tutorial" (strongest signals) | +3 |

**Why This Matters:**

| Topic Type | Lifespan | Example |

| Evergreen | Years | "How to edit YouTube videos for beginners" |
| News/Trending | Days/Weeks | "YouTube algorithm update December 2025" |

A video with evergreen keywords will get views as long as the topic exists. "How to introduce yourself on YouTube" will get views as long as people start YouTube channels. That's forever.
